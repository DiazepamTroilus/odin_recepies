Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, one of my fav indian dishes

Butter chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular Indian dish known for its rich and flavorful tomato-based sauce. Here's a simple recipe for making butter chicken at home:

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup tomato puree

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers and place them on a baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. You can also grill the chicken for a smoky flavor.
  3. Prepare the Sauce: In a large pan, heat ghee or butter over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook for another 2 minutes, stirring frequently.
  4. Simmer the Sauce: Add tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the spices. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Combine Chicken and Sauce: Add the baked or grilled chicken pieces to the sauce, stirring to coat them evenly. Stir in garam masala and kasuri methi (if using). Adjust salt and spice levels according to your taste.
